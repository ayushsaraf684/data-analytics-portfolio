## 1. Lending Club Credit Risk & Borrower Behaviour Analysis
`Python` `Pandas` `EDA` `Feature Engineering`

### The honest story of this project

I found this dataset on the US government database. 10,000 real borrower records. 26+ columns. The moment I opened it, I wanted to close it. I spent two full days just sitting with the data. Overwhelmed, not sure where to start, genuinely considering dropping it.

I did not drop it.

Instead, I did something I had not done before. I learned the domain first. FICO scores. Credit utilization ratios. Loan grades. How a person's financial past follows them into every future borrowing decision. I needed to understand the business before I could understand the data.

Then I built the analysis in layers. First understanding single variables. Then finding relationships between two variables. Then engineering **four new columns from scratch** that I felt would reveal cleaner patterns than what was already available.

### Questions explored

- What separates a low risk borrower from a high risk one?
- How do income levels interact with loan grades?
- Does credit behaviour history predict default better than income?
- Can borrowers be segmented into meaningful risk groups?

### Key findings

- Income alone is a weak predictor of repayment
- Credit grade combined with delinquency history was the strongest risk signal
- Recent delinquency acted as an early warning
- Certain borrower clusters showed extreme risk concentration

## 2. Global Retail Sales Analytics
`SQL Server` `T-SQL` `Window Functions` `Business Intelligence`

A deep SQL project on retail sales data with 60,000+ rows across multiple tables.

### Questions explored

- Which products drive revenue and which destroy margin?
- Which stores outperform others?
- Are there seasonal revenue patterns?
- Which customers return and which do not?

### SQL techniques used

- Window functions (`DENSE_RANK`, `LAG`, cumulative `SUM`)
- Multi table joins across customers, orders, products and stores
- Time analysis using `DATEDIFF` and `YEAR`
- Working with imperfect data without deleting records

## 3. Bank Customer Churn Analysis
`Power BI` `Business Analytics` `Dashboard Design`

Most churn dashboards answer the question: *how many customers left?*

This project tries to answer a deeper question. *Which customers leaving actually hurts the business?*

### Key findings

- High balance customers were churning more than expected
- Certain product lines had structural churn problems
- Germany had the highest risk geography
- Credit score had almost no relationship with churn
