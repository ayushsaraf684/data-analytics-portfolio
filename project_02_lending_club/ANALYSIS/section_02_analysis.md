# Section 2 — Credit Behaviour, Utilization & Delinquency Risk Analysis

---

## Section 2.1 — Credit Utilization, Stress & Credit Event Impact

### Credit Utilization Risk

- When borrowers use **more than 75% of available credit**, risk increases sharply:
  - **~99.99% of loans show some risk**
  - Average risk score ~**34.1**
  - **34% fall into high-risk category (50+ score)**  

- In comparison, borrowers using **less than 75% of credit** show:
  - ~42–44% risky loans  
  - Average risk score ~13.5  
  - Only ~7% high risk  

This confirms that **high credit utilization is a strong early risk signal**.

---

### Credit Stress Population Split

- About **24.5% of loans (2,452 loans)** come from credit-stressed borrowers.  
- These borrowers show:
  - **3× higher average risk** (34.57 vs 10.11)  
  - **11× higher high-risk probability** (34% vs 3%)

---

### Credit Utilization vs Debt Mix

- High Credit Utilization borrowers:
  - 100% show risk  
  - Average risk ~34  
  - ~33–34% high risk  

- High debt but low utilization borrowers show **moderate risk levels**, meaning:
  - Credit behaviour matters more than total debt amount.

---

### Severe Credit Event Impact

- **25.7% of loans** have severe events like bankruptcy or collections.  
- These borrowers show:
  - **3.5× higher average risk** (34.24 vs 9.85 clean records)  
  - **11× higher high-risk probability** (33% vs 3%)

---

### Combined Warning Signals

When multiple flags exist together (High DTI + High Utilization + Delinquency + Severe Events):
- Average risk often crosses **40+ risk score range**.

---

## Strategic Takeaways

**Priority Segment:**  
Low utilization (<50%), clean credit history, and no stress signals consistently show lowest risk.

**High Caution Segment:**  
Borrowers with utilization >75% or severe credit events carry **5–11× baseline risk** and require stricter monitoring or pricing adjustments.

**Key Insight:**  
Credit behaviour predicts risk better than debt size.  
A borrower maxing out ₹5K limit is riskier than one managing ₹50K responsibly.

**Portfolio Level View:**  
~25% of loans contain stress signals and require tighter monitoring rules.

## Section 2.2 — Delinquency Timing & Behaviour Recovery

### Recency of Delinquency Matters Most

- Borrowers with delinquency in last 12 months:
  - ~34.13% High Risk  

- Borrowers clean for 2+ years:
  - Risk falls close to never-default borrowers  
  - ~6.74% vs ~5.12%

---

### The Highest Risk Group — “Toxic Combo”

- 331 borrowers show:
  - Recent Delinquency  
  - High Credit Stress  

- Result:
  - **100% High Risk Rate**
  - These loans behave like guaranteed loss segments.

---

### Credit Shopping vs Payment Failure

- Recent Delinquency → Massive risk jump (~34%)  
- High Credit Inquiries → Mild risk increase  
  - Even extreme inquiries (10+) → ~12.5% risk  

This suggests:
- Penalize missed payments strongly  
- Don’t over-penalize credit shopping behaviour.

---

## Key Behaviour Insights (Summary)

- Recent delinquency drives risk far more than historical issues.  
- Risk remains elevated for ~24 months after delinquency.  
- Delinquency + Utilization stress is the most dangerous borrower profile.  
- High credit activity alone is not a crisis indicator.
