# Section 5 — Homeownership, Verification & Stability Risk Signals

---

## Homeownership & Risk Patterns

### Renters — Highest Risk Segment

- Highest average risk score  
- Highest share of high-risk loan amount  

Renters consistently show higher portfolio risk exposure compared to other ownership groups.

---

### Mortgage Holders — Safest Segment

- Lowest overall risk intensity  
- Lowest high-risk loan proportion  

Mortgage ownership appears to be a strong stability indicator.

---

## Verification Impact by Homeownership Type

- **Mortgage Borrowers:**  
  Income verification slightly reduces risk further.

- **Renters:**  
  Even verified renters show higher risk compared to other groups.

- **Source Verified Renters:**  
  Show unexpectedly higher risk — requires deeper investigation in future analysis.

---

## Mortgage vs Non-Mortgage Borrowers

- Mortgage borrowers are slightly safer overall.  
- Non-mortgage borrowers show higher sensitivity to verification quality.

This suggests verification becomes more critical when asset backing (like home ownership) is absent.

---

## Job Tenure + Homeownership Interaction

- New Job + Own Home → Slightly elevated risk  
- Unknown Tenure + Renting → Highest risk combination  
- Long Tenure + Mortgage → Safest borrower profile  

---

## Strategic Suggestions

### Monitoring for Renters
Apply slightly tighter screening and monitoring for renter profiles (not rejection-based, but risk-aware review).

---

### Strengthen Verification for Non-Mortgage Borrowers
Ensure strong income and employment verification for borrowers without mortgage backing.

---

### Extra Checks for Unknown Job Tenure
Unknown or unstable employment history should trigger additional validation checks.
