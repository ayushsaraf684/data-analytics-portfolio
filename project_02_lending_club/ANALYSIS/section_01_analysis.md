# Section 1 — Big Picture Portfolio Health

## How Healthy Is the Lending Portfolio Today?

---

## Portfolio Health & Performance

- **93.75% of loans are current**, with a very low **0.07% charge-off rate**, showing strong overall portfolio stability.  
- The portfolio contains **~$163M across 10,000 loans**, with very low delinquency levels.

---

## Risk Distribution

- **88.74% of loans** fall within **risk score 0–25**, representing **89.92% of total portfolio value**.  
- **52.41% of loans show some risk signal** (49.14% of total value), suggesting risk is widely distributed but generally moderate.  
- **Less than 1% of the portfolio** falls into very high risk (75+ risk score).

---

## Grade Analysis

- **Grades A–C dominate the portfolio**, contributing more than **81% of total loans**.  
- **Grade A alone holds 50.86% of total portfolio value** at the lowest risk level.  
- **Grade B leads in loan volume** with **3,037 loans** and about **$49.3M exposure**.  
- Lower grades (D–G) show progressively higher risk concentration.

---

## Income vs Risk Relationship

- **Low-income borrowers (≤ $40k)** show:
  - **55.07% risky loan count**
  - Average risk score of **16.57**

- **High-income borrowers (≥ $100k)** show:
  - Lower overall risk
  - Average risk score of **14.79**

- **Grade A borrowers earn ~32% more than Grade E borrowers**, supporting current credit grading logic.

---

## Loan Size Risk Dynamics

- **Large loans (≥ $30k)** appear safest:
  - ~42% risky rate  
  - Average risk score ~12.98  

- **Small-mid loans ($5k – $10k)** show highest risk:
  - ~55–58% risky rate  
  - Risk score ~17–18  

- **Mid-to-large loans contribute most to total portfolio risk exposure** due to higher portfolio share.

---

## Strategic Recommendations

### Focus Growth on Strong Segments
Higher credit grades (A–C), borrowers earning **≥ $70k**, and larger loan sizes (**≥ $30k**) show stronger stability.  
These segments should be prioritized for controlled portfolio expansion.

---

### Reduce Exposure in High-Risk Segments
Borrowers with:
- Income **≤ $40k**
- Loan size **< $10k**

show higher risk concentration.  
Stricter approval checks and tighter monitoring can help control future losses.

---

### Monitor Early Risk Signals Actively
Even though current defaults are very low, risk signals exist across all grades.  
Tracking these signals early can help prevent future default build-up.

---

## Overall Conclusion

The portfolio is currently performing well with very low realized losses.  
However, since **~52% of loans show some level of risk**, proactive and targeted risk monitoring across borrower segments is necessary to maintain long-term portfolio performance.
