# Section 4 — Credit Usage Behaviour & Borrowing Patterns

---

## Revolving Depth (Card Usage Intensity)

### Normal Card Users (2–3 Cards) — Highest Risk Segment

- ~21% high-risk loans — highest among all card usage groups  
- Highest average risk score (~24.99)  

This segment represents a **key early warning group** despite not appearing extreme.

---

### Income vs Card Count Relationship

- Income increases as number of credit cards increases.  
- However, risk does **not increase proportionally**.  

Extreme users (10+ cards):
- Highest income group  
- Only moderate risk levels  

This suggests **higher income may offset card load risk** to some extent.

---

### Safest Segment — Transactors (Pay in Full)

- Lowest average risk score  
- Lowest high-risk loan percentage  

Shows strong repayment discipline.

---

### Heavy Card Users (7+ Cards)

- Lower risk than expected  
- Possibly financially experienced or high liquidity borrowers  

---

## Borrowing Behaviour (Loan Count Pattern)

### Borrowing Frequency vs Risk

- Risk decreases as borrowing frequency increases.  
- High frequency borrowers (10+ loans) show ~7% high-risk rate.

This suggests:
- Easier credit access = lender confidence = lower observed risk.

---

### DTI vs Borrowing Behaviour

- DTI increases with borrowing count.  
- But risk does not increase accordingly.  

This indicates **DTI alone is not a strong risk predictor** in this dataset.

---

## Cross Pattern Insights

- Risk is **behaviour driven more than income driven**.  
- Moderate usage segments (not extreme or minimal) often show highest stress.  
- Credit access appears correlated with lower default risk probability.

---

## Strategic Recommendations

### Focus Monitoring on Normal Card Users (2–3 Cards)

- Add additional verification checks  
- Apply slightly stricter approval thresholds  
- Implement early post-disbursement monitoring  

---

### Avoid Over-Penalizing High Card Count Customers

Instead evaluate based on:
- Income stability  
- Repayment behaviour consistency  
- Credit utilization pattern  

---

### Combine Credit History With Behaviour Signals

Instead of approving based only on long credit history, use:
- Long credit history  
- Low delinquency history  
- Stable credit utilization behaviour  

---

## Key Takeaway

Borrower behaviour patterns provide stronger risk signals than income or credit limits alone.  
Moderate usage segments require closer monitoring compared to extreme or minimal usage groups.
